Six Reasons to Own This Asset

Largest Rent Upside in the Submarket

In-place average rent of $1,400/unit is below every active rent comp on Sumac Lane and Nutwood Street. Mark-to-market on natural turnover drives ~39% loss-to-lease capture without entitlements or major capex.

Below Replacement & Below Comp Basis

Costa-Hawkins Friendly Operating Environment

Anaheim has no local rent stabilization ordinance. State AB 1482 applies (5%+CPI cap, just-cause evictions) but vacancy decontrol remains intact — owners reset rents to market on every turnover.

Stabilized Family Workforce Submarket

92804 features 86,000+ residents at 3.35 persons/household — a deep, stable workforce-rental tenant base anchored by the Anaheim Resort District, West Anaheim Medical Center, and Disneyland-area employment (21%+ of city employment per Anaheim's 2024 ACFR).

Latent Density & 8-ADU State Pathway

RM-4 zoning permits 36 units by-right on the 1-acre parcel — 12 units of latent density above the existing 24. Additionally, the new California state ADU program permits up to 8 detached ADUs on a multifamily site of this size, layering a third value-add channel on top of mark-to-market and renovation (subject to municipal review).

Clean Operating Record

Anaheim Quality Rental Housing Program inspections in 2015, 2021, 2022, and 2025 — all closed cleanly. No active liens, demolition orders, or chronic violations. Roofs replaced 2002–2003; consistent owner-managed maintenance posture.

Pricing Rationale
Why $5.8M Reflects the Right Value

Subject is priced at $5,800,000 ($241,667/unit, $237/SF) — a positioning that delivers a defensible asking basis and meaningful value-add narrative for the OC private-capital buyer pool.

On a per-unit basis, the list price is below every verified Tier 1+2 sold comp in the submarket and 14.7% below the closest active peer (El Pueblo at 401 S Beach, also 1978 vintage). On a per-SF basis, $237 sits below the $345–$405/SF comp range — the explanation is unit size: subject's 1,019 SF average is materially larger than the comp set's 692–912 SF, and per-SF metrics compress predictably as units scale up.

On a cap-rate basis, the headline 4.13% in-place yield falls below the comp range (4.78%–5.50%) only because subject is uniquely under-rented. In-place average rent of $1,400/unit is below every comp's underlying rent profile — a 39% loss-to-lease versus validated $1,950–$2,400 market rents on Sumac Lane and Nutwood Street. At full mark-to-market, the property re-prices to a 7.62% cap on the same in-place SF and existing operations, exceeding the comp range ceiling without entitlements, capital improvements, or repositioning capex.

The trade range of $5,600,000 – $5,950,000 reflects a 50bp cap-rate spread around list. Conservative qualified buyers underwrite to a stabilized 6.0–6.5% yield to justify acquisition basis; more aggressive value-add operators with proven OC rental execution can stretch closer to the high end of the range.

TERTIARY VALUE-ADD · NOT MODELED IN ABOVE RETURNS

Layered Upside: 8 Detached ADUs Under SB 1211

California Senate Bill 1211 (Skinner, Chapter 296, signed September 19, 2024) expanded the state ADU program for multifamily properties. On an existing multifamily site, the statute authorizes up to 8 detached ADUs (not to exceed the number of existing dwelling units) plus additional conversion ADUs within non-livable space — all under ministerial approval (no discretionary review). Side/rear setbacks for ADU construction are capped at 4 feet, and local agencies cannot require off-street parking replacement when garages or carports are demolished for ADU construction.

Applied to Del Rey Apartments (24 existing units, 1-acre RM-4 lot): the property qualifies for the full 8-detached-ADU allowance, plus up to ~6 additional conversion ADUs from non-livable space (storage, mechanical rooms, etc., at the statutory cap of 25% of existing units). ADUs are typically delivered as 1-bedroom flats or studios (~400–550 SF) at a development cost of approximately $200,000–$275,000 per unit, with stabilized rents in line with the submarket's validated 1BR market rents ($1,800–$1,950/month per the rent-comp set).

At full execution, an 8-ADU expansion adds approximately $184,000 of incremental annual GSR (8 × $1,925 × 12) and roughly $130,000 of incremental NOI on a stabilized basis, supporting an additional ~$2.5 million of asset value at a 5.25% exit cap — meaningful but separate from the renovation case. ADU delivery requires standalone capital and design / permitting timeline (estimated 12–18 months under SB 1211's ministerial framework).

SB 1211 (Govt. Code §65852.2 et seq., as amended) is a state preemption — local agencies must ministerially approve compliant ADU applications. ADU yield remains subject to site-plan engineering (utilities, fire access, sewer / water capacity); buyer to verify with the Anaheim Planning Department prior to underwriting ADU upside in the bid basis.
